# 📖 The AI Demystification Glossary

AI is full of big words that mean simple things. We strip away the complexity so you can understand the technology on a human level.

---

### **LLM (Large Language Model)**
A type of AI (like ChatGPT) that has been trained on massive amounts of text to predict the next word in a sequence. It doesn't "know" things like a human does; it calculates probabilities.

### **Prompt**
The instruction or question you give to an AI. Think of it as "natural language programming."

### **Token**
The "currency" of an LLM. AI doesn't read words; it reads tokens (pieces of words or characters). For example, "Project" might be 1 token, but "antidisestablishmentarianism" might be several.

### **Hallucination**
When an AI confidently provides information that is factually incorrect or completely fabricated. This happens because the AI is a probability engine, not a database.

### **Context Window**
The amount of information the AI can "remember" during your current conversation. If you talk for too long, the AI might "forget" things you said at the start.

### **Fine-Tuning**
The process of taking a pre-trained AI and giving it extra training on a specific topic (like legal documents or medical data) to make it an expert in that area.

### **Weights**
Internal numbers in an AI model that determine its behavior. "Learning" is the process of adjusting these billions of numbers until the AI's predictions are accurate.

### **Transformer**
The specific mathematical architecture that allows modern AI to understand the relationship between words across long distances. (See [Deep AI Foundations](../resources/foundation.md)).

### **RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)**
A method where humans rank the AI's answers to teach it what is helpful, polite, and safe.
